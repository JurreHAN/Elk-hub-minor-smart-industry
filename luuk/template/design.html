Design Process

Creating a creative 3D printed enclosure.

A more in-depth look into the design and 3D printing process of the custom enclosure for my Smart Plant Watering System.

Printing Specs

  • Bambu Lab A1
  • 0.2mm Layer Height
  • 210°C Nozzle / 60°C Bed
  • PLA Filament (Black and White)

Design Goals

  • Compact & sleek design
  • Keep components accessible
  • Case locked securely
  • Custom fit for all components

The Idea

During the PCB design workshop, I created a compact design for the Smart Plant Watering System. So I also wanted to keep the enclosure as compact as possible while maintaining accessibility for maintenance. At first, it was a challenge to come up with a design that checked all the boxes. Designing a case that could fit all the components was not a problem at all. However, keeping the enclose compact and aesthetic while still allowing easy access to the electronics proved to be tricky. Luckily, after several iterations, I came up with a two-part case with a sliding lid mechanism. By using magnets to attach the lid to the base, I could ensure that the case remained securely closed during operation. This final design allowed easy access to the electronics while keeping them securely enclosed.

Designing in Fusion 360

The first thing I did was measuring the PCB and components using a digital caliper. On top of that, I used the datasheets to get the exact dimensions of the a few components. With these measurements, I started modeling the enclosure in Fusion 360. I created a base that snugly fits the PCB and components, ensuring there were cutouts for connectors, components and ventilation. The lid was designed to slide in the base using a dovetail joint to keep it in place securely. It was important to add some tolerances to the design to account for any inaccuracies during printing and measuring. To my suprise, the first print fit perfectly with only a few things on the slide part that needed slight adjustments to align correctly. After printing 2 extra test pieces of the back of the case, the design was finished. I printed the whole slide part of the enclosure again and everything fitted perfectly.

During assembly, I bumped into a few small issues. One of them was that some cables were to long. To solve this, I cut a few cables to the right length and resoldered them. The end result is a sleek, compact enclosure that not only protects the electronics but also is a pleasure to use and look at.
